how to roll a joint
First you must select the right papers. This is entirely a matter of personal preference. Favourites include King Size Smoking, Slimline Kingsize Rizla Blue, and King Size Zig Zag. Next you must select a construction technique, of which there are hundreds. The method explained here is a variety from the 'L-shape' joint family. Collect together your paraphernalea and place them on a flat surface like a table, book or album cover. Hold two papers gum side up, one horizontally so the gum is away from you, the other vertically. Lick the gum on the lower half of the vertical paper, and stick it to the underside of the horisontal paper. The papers should only overlap on the gum itself. Refold the horizontal paper along its length, extending the fold to the other paper. This simply enables the ingredients to sit more easily on the papers while the joint is being made. Next you may decide to reduce the amount of paper at the fat end, this is simply to reduce the amount of paper smoke. There are a variety of ways to do this. Here we have removed a triangle of paper from the top end of the gum to halfway along the length on the opposite side.
Next you will need a roach. Suitable roach materials include, focus points, cigarette boxes, train tickets, etc. The most commonly used material is cigarette paper packets. Though club flyers come a close second, as club flyers are specifically designed to make good roach material, compelling people to take them home rather than discard them. Rip of a section of roach material. The size will depend on the thickness and personal preference. Roll it tightly. You may wish to unroll it and repeat the exersize a few times. This is often necessary if it is quite thick, which may cause it to crease rather than roll smoothly. Place the roach at the thin end. Often, many people do not insert the roach until the end, after the spliff has been rolled. Again, this is purely a matter of choice, and you should use whichever technique you find most comfortable. Now begin to loosly sprinkle tobacco along the crease. The tobacco you use is entirely up to you, and with so many brands on the market you are spoilt for choice. You can go for cigarette tobacco or rolling tobacco. Many people who don't smoke cigarettes use brands like Silk Cut Ultra, or even non-tobacco smoking blends, the primary ingredient of which is marshmallow.
Now it's time for the gear. There is a bewildering variety of cannabis available; split into two main types. Cannabis Resin (solid, hash, black, soap, etc.) needs to be burned slightly and then sprinkled in. Big lumps are to be avoided as these can cause hot rock burns (red hot lumps of cannabis that fall from the joint and land on your T-shirt, new coat, favourite pants, hand, or eye; burning a searing hole through it). The other form cannabis takes is grass or bush, which is purely the leaves of the plant. Here we are using Skunk, a potent variety of cannabis in leaf and bud form. Skunk needs to be pulled apart, usually between finger nails. Many people use skunk scissors to cut it into little pieces. Try to give an even distribution of cannabis, avoiding clumping or gaps. It is bad smoking ettiquette to fill the beginning of a spliff and scrimp on the rest in order to get more yourself. Now all the ingredients are in you may want to rearange things slightly if anything looks lumpy or otherwise awkward to roll. Now hold the joint between your thumbs and forefingers. Slide the papers up and down against each other so the tobacco is gently rolled. This procedure is no different to rolling a cigarette. Take care to ensure the roach stays in place at this point.
Roll the non-gummed edge away from you so that it tucks down underneath the tobacco. The papers may need some coaxing at this point. With an upward motion of your thumb and a downward motion of your fingers, roll the joint up until the gum is nearly touching the now rolled paper. Carefully lick the gum. Try not to slobber all over the joint, as this might make the gum too wet and unable to stick properly, or in extreme cases may soak the tobacco and make the joint useless. If you do find yourself slobbering over the spliff, and the problem persists, consult a doctor. Continue rolling the joint so that the gum sticks the paper down. Give the joint a careful run over with your fingers to ensure it is all in place. Congratulations, the hard part is over. Now you will need the proddy thing from a Clipper lighter. The Clipper lighter is possibly one of the greatest tools known to man, far superior to the Swiss Army knife. It can not only light your spliff for you but also help you build it. If a Clipper is not available, you will have to root around for a matchstick, pen top, hair pin, nail or other inferior prodding object.
Use the Clipper tool to gently press the tobacco in to the end of the spliff. This compactness will stop the smoke being too harsh and stop the joint from burning too quickly. Hold the loos paper at the end of the joint in one hand and the rest of the joint in the other. Twist the loose paper tightly so that there is no space between the contents of the spliff and the twist itself. After some practice you may be able to do this with one hand. Bite off the twist of paper. This is not essential, but will ensure the joint burns more evenly once lit. Use your front teeth in a simple cutting motion, you will be surprised how easy this is.

Your spliff should now be successfully constructed. Don't worry if it looks a little wrinkly, bulges in the middle or is bent. You will soon get the hang of building the 'perfect' spliff with a little practice. You may even wish to discover different techniques or even try inventing your own.

Light and enjoy.
